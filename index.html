🔍 Can anticipatory bail be granted after FIR is filed in 498A cases? Researched in 1.2s
94 Confidence · 12 cases analyzed · 3 statutes

Short Answer

Yes. Anticipatory bail under Section 438 CrPC can be granted even after an FIR is filed, including in 498A cases, subject to the court's discretion on gravity and prima facie case.

Ratio Decidendi

The Constitution Bench in Sushila Aggarwal v. State (NCT) (2020) held that:

  1. No blanket time limit on anticipatory bail — protection extends through trial
  2. Court considers gravity of offence + cooperation with investigation
  3. Custodial interrogation is not always required for serious offences

Is a non-compete clause without geographic limitation enforceable under Indian Law?
Researched in 1.8s
92% Confidence  ·  18 cases analyzed  ·  5 statutes

Short Answer

A non-compete clause without geographic limitation is generally unenforceable in India if it imposes an unreasonable restriction on trade, profession, or business, as it would be against public policy under Section 27 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872.

Key Reasoning

  • Section 27 voids agreements that restrain lawful trade.
  • Courts require restrictions to be reasonable in scope, duration, and geography.
  • Overbroad clauses are typically read down or struck down.
⚖️ Illustrative Case
Superintendence Co. Pvt. Ltd. v. Krishan Murgai (1989) 2 SCC 409
The Supreme Court held that restrictions must be reasonable and not injurious to public interest.
